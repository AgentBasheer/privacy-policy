Privacy Policy

Last Updated: August 31, 2025

Introduction

Welcome to DarkBrowser ("the App"). We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy outlines how we handle your information when you use our private browser application.

By using the App, you agree to this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

Information We Collect

Our core principle is privacy. We do not collect personally identifiable information.

Local Storage

To enhance your experience, the App saves settings locally on your device. This data is never sent to our servers. This includes:

  • Theme and privacy preferences
  • Bookmarks and favorites
  • Tab and extension settings

Analytics Data

We use OneSignal (for notifications) and Firebase (for analytics) to collect limited, anonymous data to improve the App. This includes device type, usage stats, and crash reports. This data cannot identify you personally.

How We Use Information

We use the limited, anonymous data solely to:

  • Provide and maintain App functionality
  • Improve and optimize performance
  • Understand user interaction to guide development
  • Diagnose and fix technical issues
  • Send optional push notifications
Information Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information. We do not share personally identifiable information because we do not collect it.

Third-Party Services

Our analytics partners operate under their own privacy policies:

  • OneSignal: Privacy Policy
  • Firebase: Privacy Policy
In-App Purchases

We offer optional in-app purchases and subscriptions to support the App's development.

  • Purchases are for support only and do not unlock extra features.
  • All purchases are final and non-refundable.
  • Transactions are processed securely by the Google Play Store.
  • You can manage subscriptions in your Google Play account.
Your Rights & Data Control

You have full control over your data:

  • Access: View locally stored data in the App's settings.
  • Deletion: Clear all local data by uninstalling the App or clearing its data in your device settings.
  • Object: Opt out of analytics via your device's privacy settings.
